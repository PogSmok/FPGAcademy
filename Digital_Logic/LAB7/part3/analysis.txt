Absolutely. Instead of using two separate shift registers, the design can use a single 4-bit shift register
and simply check its contents against the patterns 4'b0000 (indicating that `w` has been low for four consecutive
clock cycles) or 4'b1111 (indicating that `w` has been high for four consecutive clock cycles).

A minor drawback of this approach is that an asynchronous reset initializes the shift register to
4'b0000, which would also satisfy the detection condition for four zeros.
However, this does not invalidate the design: outputs during reset are not considered valid detections,
and the purpose of the asynchronous reset is to bring the system into a known starting state, which it successfully does.
After reset is released, the detector functions exactly as intended.
